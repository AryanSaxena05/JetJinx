Explore the core modules powering Boiler Jet Jinx: Machine Learning, Optimization, and Graph Analysis. Each module features a clear methodology and actionable insights.

Machine Learning Module

Methodology
  • Data & Labeling: 2015–2019 BTS On‑Time dataset (~1.2 M rows); IS_DELAYED=1 if arrival > 15 min late.
  • Preprocessing: Drop cancelled/diverted flights; impute route‑median delays. Normalize timestamps (UTC → hour/day/month); cyclical encoding. One‑hot encode carrier/origin/destination; standard‑scale numeric features.
  • Feature Engineering: Rolling 30‑day on‑time rates (carrier & route). Distance categories (quantiles) & 7‑day lagged-delay history.
  • Modeling & Tuning: Pipeline: Logistic Regression (baseline), Random Forest, XGBoost. Bayesian 5‑fold CV over depth, learning rate, class‑weights.
  • Evaluation: 80/20 stratified hold‑out (20 000 records). Metrics: Accuracy 82.5 %, Precision 0.83, Recall 0.81, F1 0.82, ROC AUC 0.88. SHAP analysis to rank top predictors.
Insights
  • Key Drivers: Scheduled Departure Time, Carrier Reliability, Flight Distance & Route patterns.
  • Business Impact: 5 % lift in AUC could save airlines millions via optimized staffing and proactive rebooking.
  • Operational Recommendation: Flag high‑risk flights (> 60 % delay probability) in daily manifests; integrate real‑time alerts into passenger apps.

Optimization Module (LP & DP)

Methodology
Delay‑Reduction LP/IP:
  • Variables: \( x_i \) = minutes reduced per delay type.
  • Objective:
    $$ \max \sum_i x_i $$
  • Subject to constraints:
    • Budget: $$\sum_i w_i x_i \leq B$$ (weights \( w_i \) inversely ∝ historical frequency)
    • Per-type cap: $$x_i \leq 0.6 \times \text{historical average}$$
    • Proportional cap: $$x_i \leq 0.4 T$$
    • Binary gating for weather delay inclusion
  • Solve across budgets 1–200 to detect regime shifts.
Multi‑City Routing DP:
  • Bitmask + memoization for TSP on {SFO, DEN, ATL, JFK}.
  • Distance matrix from flight data; DP finds min‑mile tour in $$O(2^n n^2)$$.
Insights
  • LP Regimes: 1–108 units: prioritize airline & late‑aircraft; 109–111: add security; 112–144: swap in weather; 145–200: all five types; plateau at 166 units (35.35 min saved).
  • TSP Savings: Optimal order SFO→DEN→ATL→JFK cuts consultant travel by ~35 % (2,926 mi total).

Graph Analysis Module

Methodology
  • Network Construction: Top 30 U.S. airports as nodes; edges weighted by flight counts (directed) or avg. distance (undirected).
  • Centrality & Reachability: Degree Centrality (networkx); hop-distance matrix; heatmap of min‑flight hops.
  • Lean Network (MST): Prim's algorithm on undirected distance graph → 29‑edge minimum spanning tree.
  • Resilience Testing: Remove hubs (ATL/ORD/LAX); measure PageRank before/after to expose regional dependencies.
Insights
  • Super‑Hubs: ATL & DEN connect to all peers (degree = 2.0) → critical for national throughput.
  • Connectivity: > 90 % of airport pairs reachable in ≤ 2 hops → high redundancy.
  • MST Structure: 29 routes achieve full coverage; urban clusters (PHL–LGA–BOS) vs. long‑hauls (DAL–BNA).
  • Structural Fragility: HNL's PageRank drops > 10 % when LAX is removed—flag for contingency planning.
